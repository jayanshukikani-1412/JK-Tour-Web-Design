Uttarakhand

Explore The Unexplored

A mystical land of mountains and mythologies, exquisite landscapes and exhilarating adventure, and wellness and yoga, Uttarakhand has something to offer every traveller. Popularly known as Devbhoomi, or the land of gods, the state is framed by the Himalayas and divided into two main regions, Garhwal and Kumaon. While Uttarakhand has several well-known destinations like Nainital, Mussoorie, Corbett National Park and Auli, and pilgrimage sites like Kedarnath, Badrinath, Rishikesh and Haridwar, explore the 13 lesser-known destinations from the state's 13 districts.

Tips for Travellers for Planning their Tour to Uttarakhand

  • Choose the right time to visit Uttarakhand. The time between October and June is the best.
  • Carry your identity proof and few copies in case you are planning to a destination where permission/permit is required.
  • Winters can be really harsh, therefore carry heavy woollens if visiting between October and March.
  • There are many high-altitude destinations in Uttarakhand where breathing problems and altitude sickness are common. Make sure to take the necessary medicines when travelling.
  • The accommodation options in Uttarakhand in many. In remote destinations, homestay options and camping are also available.
  • Before planning a trip to Uttarakhand, read about the states policy about birds and animals, entering sacred places, eco-tourism, and interacting with tribals.
  • In monsoons, the rains can be heavy, therefore, it is advised to check the weather forecast before travelling.
